# FleetMind Evaluation Vehicle Manual

## Red Engine-Temperature Warning

A red engine-temperature warning indicates a potentially dangerous overheating condition. The driver must stop the vehicle in a safe location as soon as practical, switch off the engine, engage the parking brake, and contact fleet maintenance.

The driver must not continue operating the vehicle until an authorized technician has inspected and cleared it for service. Opening the coolant reservoir while the engine is hot is prohibited because pressurized coolant can cause serious burns.

## Yellow Battery Warning

A yellow battery warning indicates that the charging system may require inspection. When no smoke, burning smell, or loss of electrical power is present, the driver may complete the current trip and report the warning before the next dispatch.

If the warning is accompanied by smoke, a burning smell, dimming lights, or repeated electrical failures, the driver must stop safely, switch off the vehicle, and contact fleet maintenance.

## Tire Inspection

Before each shift, the driver must inspect all tires for visible damage, abnormal wear, embedded objects, and insufficient inflation. A tire with exposed reinforcement material, a sidewall bulge, or a deep cut must be removed from service.

The driver must record the defect in the vehicle inspection report and notify the fleet supervisor.

## Maintenance Reporting

Safety-critical warnings must be reported immediately by telephone or through the approved fleet-maintenance system. Non-critical observations may be included in the end-of-shift vehicle report.

Every report must include the vehicle identifier, warning or defect observed, approximate time, current location, and any action already taken.
